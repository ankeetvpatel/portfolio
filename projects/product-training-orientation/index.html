Complex Product Training | eLearning

This eLearning-based complex product training helps a financial services organization orient its internal and external agents to understand a financial product in-depth.

  • Role: Instructional Design, Learning Experience Design
  • Target Audience: Financial Services Agents
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Adobe Illustrator, Microsoft PowerPoint
  • Client: A fictional financial services organization (name not disclosed to respect the client's non-disclosure agreement)

Overview

A medium-sized financial services organization had plans to launch a new product - a home improvement loan. However, a key step before the launch was to ensure that all the in-house and external agents were abreast with the new product.

The currently available training material was found to less effective as almost 40% of agents were unable to clear the post product training assessment. The Sales Manager contacted me to analyze the training material. The manager, who was also the Subject Matter Expert (SME), and I agreed that there were three key pitfalls in the training material.

One: the material was overloaded with unnecessary details that the agents on the ground would never need. Two: the training was filled with key financial information but was devoid of context or real-world scenarios for learners. Three: the training only focused on 'knowing' but not much on the 'doing' part of learning.

To address the above, I proposed an eLearning-based product training with a locked linear navigation where the next module only unlocks after the previous one has been completed. This progressive style of learning ensures that the learners would first uncover basic information related to the financial loan product, before they explore the key processes to apply for the loan. Once they are through the first two modules, they would then be exposed to common customer scenarios, where they get to make key decisions related to serving the loan applicants.

Process

I worked closely with the SME to ideate on, design and develop this interactive eLearning product orientation. The following were the key stages:

  • Categorizing content into progressive modules
  • Rapid prototyping
  • Developing the eLearning package in Storyline

Categorizing Content into Progressive Modules

From the very beginning of the project, it was imperative to chunk this long-form technical content into modules. The original training documents combined with key insights from the SME served as input for this exercise. After long deliberations and discussions, it was decided that the content should move the learner progressively from 'learning' about the home improvement loan to 'doing' - in other words, making key decisions pertaining to the customers' queries about this product.

Rapid Prototyping

Once the higher-level content flow was approved, it was agreed that we should initiate rapid prototyping for each module. Most importantly, I had to make sure that the client gets clarity on the functionality and interactivity available to the learners.

This allowed us the flexibility to make changes, if any, before a full-blown package was developed in Articulate Storyline with all the bells and whistles.

Developing the eLearning Package in Storyline

Once the storyboard was approved, the content was in place. This was the perfect time to draw inspiration from client's existing branding, such as their website, brochures and online advertisements to decide the color scheme and typography.

This ensured consistency of branding as the design/development continued in Articulate Storyline. Another aspect which required much thought and planning was the programming of the locked navigation menu. We had to make sure that the future sections were locked until the current section was explored completely.

A range of interactive features also were added to make this concept more engaging and less monotonous. These include click-to-reveal, flashcards, split-screen comparison, content accordions, and scored quizzes with pop-out summary screens.

Results and Takeaways

As sound instructional design principles were followed to organize the content and develop an eLearning concept, the client saw a rapid jump in agent assessment scores. This gave the much-need push required to launch the product feeling confident the internal agents and external representatives were ready to sell the home improvement loans and answer any query the prospective customers may have.
