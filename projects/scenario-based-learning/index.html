Handling Sales Objections | eLearning Simulation

This scenario-based eLearning simulation helps sales executives deal with customers' most common hesitations about buying a product or signing up for a service.

  • Role: Instructional Design, Learning Experience Design
  • Target Audience: Sales Executives
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline, Create Studio Pro (for animation videos), Mind Map Pro (for action mapping), Microsoft Word
  • Client: RankFirst Marketing (name changed to respect the client's non-disclosure agreement)

Overview

RankFirst Marketing was a new entrant into the development of CRM applications targeted towards small and medium businesses.

According to company's internal survey, two common hesitations were observed to buying the company's product. One: RankFirst was unheard of compared to other established players in the similar space. Two: most prospects had already signed up for a competitive service.

The response to these objections usually ended with offering a discount in order to lure the prospects. Heads in the Sales Department were in agreement that this tactic could lead to a double whammy: hurting brand perception in the long run and decreasing revenues in the short run.

As a result, they wanted their sales executives to recognize these risks and make plausible decisions. After understanding the nuances of this situation, I thought that the lack of knowledge and skill surrounding this topic was a problem worth solving.

I recommended a scenario-based eLearning simulation that allows sales executives to make key decisions surrounding the common objections to buying. I also proposed a touch of gamification: including red flags every time a bad decision is chosen and green flags for more plausible decisions.

This would help the target learners recognize the impact of their choices, although in a safe eLearning environment.

Process

After analyzing my proposal, the client saw value in the above-mentioned action-oriented approach and thought this would help successfully navigate the common objections revolving around closing the sale. So, we proceeded with the project.

I worked closely with the Head of Sales at RankFirst, who was also the subject matter expert (SME) for this intervention. The constant input from the SME helped me design and develop this interactive simulation in stages:

  • Making an action map
  • Drafting a text-based storyboard in PowerPoint
  • Developing a prototype and polishing the eLearning package

Making an Action Map

At the outset, the SME and I set up a kickoff meeting where I was exposed to two key elements. Firstly, the details of the internal survey, which listed common objections to closing a sale were shared. Next, key sales executives were made available for a face-to-face interview.

The above learning analysis formed the basis for drawing up an action map to identify the key actions that would improve sales team's handling of the common objections to closing the deal. With these actions laid out and the most important actions identified, our decision points for the scenario-based learning became clear.

Drafting a Text-Based Storyboard on PowerPoint

After identifying the most important actions to focus on, I worked with the SME to craft an authentic story around the actions.

After much discussion, we decided to focus the story on a new sales executive who would have to navigate his first day on the job with minimal or no guidance. He would face choices about what to say if a sales prospect is already working with a competing software vendor or how to deal with an objection connected to price or popularity of the company.

Since the learner would be driving the scenario, they would make the choices at key decision points and see corresponding consequences if they opt for a bad decision.

I encapsulated all of this in a text-based storyboard; it included the full script of the two characters, questions and decision choices, consequences for each choice, and results/feedback screen.

Developing a Prototype and Polishing the eLearning Product

Once the text-based storyboard was complete and approved, I began work on making the prototype to match the requirements of the story featuring the sales executive's first day on the job. For the prototype, pictures were used accompanying the voice-to-text narration. The prototype showcased only the key screens (using still images/illustrations) and functionality of red/green flags.

I delivered this to the client so that key stakeholders could review the functionality, layout and the flow.

The prototype was well-received by the client. Minor tweaks were suggested which were implemented to produce the final eLearning product. This featured animated videos (created in Create Studio Pro) for all the scenes along with the voice over narration and spoken dialogues for both characters.

Since the text-based storyboard and prototype were already approved, the last phase of the project was a relatively straightforward task.

Results and Takeaways

The final product was delivered to the client in time, which helped them integrate this package on their LMS. As decided during the analysis stage, the client made it mandatory for all sales executives to complete that course. This armed them with key positive performance behaviors to deal with common sales objections.

The client appreciated the fact that this eLearning intervention was conceptualized keeping the key pain points of the learner in mind. Using the performance-oriented action mapping approach, the package only focused on reinforcing the actions that people need to perform.

This personalized, meaningful and active learning method led to better decision making on the job, and the client reported a much better sales conversion rate because of, at least in part, to this eLearning simulation.
