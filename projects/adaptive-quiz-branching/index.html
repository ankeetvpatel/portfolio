Adaptive Quiz and Score-Based Branching | eLearning

This is a high-fidelity prototype of a quiz-based assessment which adapts the level of content shown to the learner based on the score they get. The project was initiated in response to a request from a client belonging to the immigration and study-abroad counselling industry.

Note that this project features placeholder content as the focus here is to showcase a working demonstration of how the programming features and score-based branching works.

  • Role: eLearning Development
  • Target Audience: Immigration and study-abroad counselors
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline
  • Client: An immigration and study-abroad consulting firm (name not shared to respect the client's non-disclosure agreement)

Overview

This concept came to life when a zonal head of an immigration and study-abroad consulting firm shared a tricky situation she was facing on the sales floor. The firm was in expansion phase and hiring numbers were up; the traditional training model they followed would not be able to keep up with the pace at which they needed the new joinees to hit the sales floor.

Considering that not all the new joinees were absolute freshers, there was a need to filter out the ones who didn't require a full-blown traditional classroom training as they were already abreast with the concepts needed to counsel their clients. This would accelerate the revenue generation process in line with the expansion.

This was a perfect scenario to demonstrate how an adaptive quiz would come to their rescue. The idea was to make the joinees take the quiz to decide whether they would be needing the full 30-day classroom orientation or they were fit for the sales floor. The score on that quiz would decide this branching.

Process

The key elements for this concept were the scoring patterns and the passing criteria. Next, deciding the question type was also imperative.

I chose the most common question type: multiple choice questions; however, I decided to give multiple score weights to each of the three options. The best option carried maximum weight and the worst option minimum weight.

Then came the passing criteria. I decided that 3 categories would work best for the prototype. If a learner scores 80% or more, they would not have to take any classroom or online refresher course, as they would be ready to join the sales floor. If a learner scores between 50% to 79%, they would need to take a short online refresher course to sharpen their blades. Lastly, if the learner scores any less than 50%, they were ideal candidates for a full-blown classroom training.

Developing the eLearning package in Storyline

As far as the appearance and layout is concerned, I decided to go with an existing template in Articulate Storyline as the focus was to show the functional aspect of this assessment.

There were two important design decisions here: how to show the rules of the quiz clearly and how to develop quiz results/feedback screen that implemented those rules.

Advanced triggers and system variables were used to store maximum score and the evaluated score after every question. This way we could transparently show the learners how much was the score/percentage and how much they were able to score.

Next, the above calculated scores were used to govern the branching to categorize learners who were ready, who needed a quick refresher and who required a full 30-day training. This was made possible by conditional triggers and using separate scenes for each possibility.

Even though the slide content was not the most important part, it had to be conversational and playful.

Results and Takeaways

The quiz prototype received positive feedback from the zonal manager.

On a personal level, this project helped me gain a better understanding of how eLearning done right can have tangible benefits. In this case, the concept was developed to help filter those who were ready to join the sales team without any classroom intervention. This meant faster revenue generation cycles.
